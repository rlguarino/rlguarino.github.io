Learning to Write

Reasons behind this blog

Published Thu, Feb 11, 2016 by Ross Guarino

I am a horrible writer and a Scriptophobe (fear of writing). I have spent the better part of the last five years earning a technical degree. In those five years I have read countless lines of code, endless pages of tiny serifed typeface and countless 80x80 pages of mono-space of documentation with the occasional piece of ascii art. I experience anxiety when I watch other people read my writing. When I do write English, I fear that what I write does not portray what it is that I am trying to get across or that my opinion is not worth sharing in the first place.

Over the past 5 years I’ve become convinced that technical skill is not what makes great programmers great. Of course being a successful programmer requires a significant amount of technical knowledge and ability but it also requires a significant amount of communication.

The difference between a tolerable programmer and a great programmer is not how many programming languages they know, and it’s not whether they prefer Python or Java. It’s whether they can communicate their ideas. - Joel Spolsky

As programmers we sometimes forget about the social aspect of our work. Unless you never share what you write someone else will probably read it. You can expect that there will be another human reading your code at some point in the future. Whether they are trying to figure out what it does or reading your documentation trying to find how to use it, someone will read it.

During the course of my degree, I’ve spent a lot of time reading and writing code but I’ve spent virtually none of that time writing any English. My writing has spent even less time in front of others than it has in front of me. Watching someone read what I’ve written has always caused me great anxiety.

On my previous team at Google comments are restricted to full sentences and that is a habit I’ve carried after my internship. Comment sentences are isolated, simple and serve a purely technical purpose. I have no experience creating an interesting and compelling narrative, keeping someone interested in what I have to share or even convincing someone that there is merit in what I have to say.

This blog will serve as a place for me to practice writing. Since the only way to get better is to practice and get feedback this will be the place for me to do both.
